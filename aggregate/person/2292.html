Events

On 21 July 448 21 Tāmmuz A. Gr. 759 Nonos became Bishop of Edessa . The dates of Hiba's exile and Nonos' first episcopate are confused here. See factoid page

After 21 July 448 21 Tāmmuz A. Gr. 759 Nonos built a sacristy in the Church of Edessa . The dates of Hiba's exile and Nonos' first episcopate are confused here. See factoid page

After 28 October A. D. 457 28 Teshri I A. Gr. 769 Nonos became Bishop of Edessa . See factoid page

After 28 October A. D. 457 28 Teshri I A. Gr. 769 Nonos built the House of John the Baptist in Edessa . See factoid page

After 28 October A. D. 457 28 Teshri I A. Gr. 769 Nonos built the House for the leperous poor of Edessa outside the gate of Beth Shemesh . See factoid page

After 28 October A. D. 457 28 Teshri I A. Gr. 769 Nonos built the House of Martyrs to Mar Cosmas and Mar Damian . See factoid page

After 28 October A. D. 457 28 Teshri I A. Gr. 769 Nonos built monasteries. See factoid page

After 28 October A. D. 457 28 Teshri I A. Gr. 769 Nonos built towers. See factoid page

After 28 October A. D. 457 28 Teshri I A. Gr. 769 Nonos built bridges. See factoid page

After 28 October A. D. 457 28 Teshri I A. Gr. 769 Nonos made the roads secure. See factoid page

In 470/1 A. Gr. 782 Nonos died. See factoid page

On 4 September A. D. 525 4 Elul A. Gr. 836 they moved the body of Asclepius from Antioch to Edessa where he was buried alongside Nonos in the the House of Mar Barlāhā . See factoid page

In December A. D. 532 Kānun I A. Gr. 844 Andreos died and was buried in the the House of Mar Barlāhā alongside the bones of Mar Nonos and Mar Asclepius See factoid page
