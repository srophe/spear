SPEAR Factoids about Simeon the Solitaire — ܫܡܥܘܢ ܝܚܝܕܝܐ

Simeon the Solitaire is monk from Amida and features in John of Ephesus' Lives of the Eastern Saints. 1

Syriaca.org URI: http://syriaca.org/person/2099

Personal Information

Name variant(s):

the blessed man See factoid page

bondsman See factoid page

Place of residence:

Symeon the Solitary resided in a convent adjoining the city of Amida. See factoid page

Simeon resided in a cell on a mountain two miles from Amida . See factoid page

Sanctity:

Simeon was a saint. See factoid page

Relationships

John of Ephesus commemorated Simeon. See factoid page

Events

Simeon planned and built his cell two miles from Amida See factoid page

Simeon becomes a solitary after twenty years of spiritual labors See factoid page

Two young men enter the cell of Simeon and are unable to leave or put down the food they have taken until Simeon releases them by prayer. See factoid page

Simeon enters city and stands firm during persecution, giving absolution and baptizing day and night while only eating food from his own labor. See factoid page

Simeon cooks and lays out food, wine, an inscribed tablet in his cell to welcome strangers. See factoid page
