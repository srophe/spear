Events

When Charisius appeared before the Council of Nicaea and repented of his Nestorianism he was admitted to communion based on a signed profession of faith. He was not re-anointed. See factoid page

The Council of Ephesus in 431 heard from Charisius that many in Lydia followed a creed by Theodore of Mopsuestia. In rejecting it and Nestorianism, the Council required only an orthodox profession of faith without the need for be re-baptism or re-anointing. See factoid page
