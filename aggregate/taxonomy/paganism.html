SPEAR Factoids about Paganism

Syriaca.org URI: http://syriaca.org/taxonomy/paganism

event: Severus wrote a letter to Caesaria discussing the freedom of a Christian to associate with people who worship idols. See factoid page

event: After five years on Chios, Kashish was ordainedas bishop of Chios by Jacob Bardaeus and two other bishops (Conon and Eugene). See factoid page

event: Abraham, Cyriac, Barhadbshabba, and Sergius abolished paganism, overthrew idolatry, uprooted altars, destroyed shrines, and cut down trees in ardent religious zeal in the high mountainous region of Asia. See factoid page

event: After his ordination Kashish traveled around Chios with John of Ephesus converting pagans to Christianity. See factoid page

event: John of Ephesus was involved in bringing about the conversion from paganism to Christianity of eighty thousand people in the provinces of Asia, Caria, Phrygia, and Lydia. This involved the founding of ninety-eight churches and twelve monasteries and the conversion of twelve synagogues to churches. See factoid page
